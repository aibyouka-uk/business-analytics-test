🎓 Official Learning Resources — the 6 leading LLM makers

Go straight to the source. Each card links the company's free learning hub and its official YouTube channel, with a recent talk from a senior leader.

Channel links always surface the latest videos; the individual talks below are snapshots from mid-2026. Video titles are as published; talks may be hosted on third-party news channels.

🤝 Where all six broadly agree

Across OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Meta, DeepSeek and Microsoft, recent talks, docs and product launches converge on a striking amount.

The frontier is shifting from chatbots to agents & reasoning

All six now ship autonomous, tool-using agents and reasoning models that "think" before answering — from Microsoft's Autopilots to OpenAI, Claude, Gemini and DeepSeek agents.

Capability still scales — but data quality and post-training matter as much as size

Bigger models help, yet all now stress better data, fine-tuning and reinforcement/reasoning training over raw parameter count.

Multimodality is the default

Text, image, audio and video in a single model is the shared direction of travel.

Reliability and hallucination remain unsolved

Everyone acknowledges benchmarks are imperfect and outputs can be confidently wrong — so human oversight is needed for high-stakes use.

Safety and responsible-AI frameworks are table stakes

Every lab publishes a safety / responsible-AI framework and supports guardrails and evaluation of some kind.

AI will reshape work — and compute is the constraint

All expect major economic impact and reskilling, and all are racing on inference cost, efficiency and the energy/compute needed to scale.

Synthesis of public statements, docs and the talks linked below, as of mid-2026. Watch each provider's channel for their latest.

⚖️ Where they genuinely diverge

The disagreements are just as telling — on openness, safety posture, timelines and even the underlying architecture.

Open weights vs closed

Meta (Llama) and DeepSeek release open-weight models anyone can run; OpenAI, Anthropic and Google keep frontier models closed (Google offers smaller open Gemma; OpenAI has released some open weights). Microsoft stays platform-neutral, hosting many. It reflects a real split on whether openness makes AI safer or riskier.

How much to foreground safety

Anthropic is the most safety-forward (interpretability, a Responsible Scaling Policy, Constitutional AI). Google DeepMind frames safety around scientific rigour and AGI. OpenAI balances a fast capability race with safety commitments. Microsoft's Mustafa Suleyman pushes "humanist superintelligence" — powerful but contained. Meta's Yann LeCun is the most sceptical that today's models pose existential risk.

AGI timelines & definitions

Amodei (Anthropic) and Altman (OpenAI) talk of "powerful AI" within a few years; Hassabis (DeepMind) is more measured and expects further breakthroughs are needed; LeCun (Meta) argues today's LLMs alone won't get there at all.

The architecture bet

Most scale transformers plus test-time reasoning (OpenAI's o-series, DeepSeek R1, Gemini "thinking", Claude). Meta's LeCun is betting on a different path — world models (JEPA) beyond next-token prediction.

Business model & moat

OpenAI and Anthropic are product+API labs; Google integrates AI into Search and Cloud; Microsoft sells an enterprise platform (Copilot) and partners; Meta open-sources to commoditise the model layer; DeepSeek competes on radically lower training and inference cost.

Geopolitics

DeepSeek's rise (China, low-cost, open) reframed the field against US labs and export-control dynamics — a fault line each navigates differently.

Positions evolve; this is a synthesis of public statements and the talks linked below, as of mid-2026.

💼 The Strategy View — what the big consultancies publish on AI

The LLM makers above show you how to build and learn AI. The major consulting firms answer a different question: how to adopt AI in a business and capture value. Each publishes free, data-rich research — here are their flagship AI reports and insight hubs.

Free to read (some ask for an email). Reports are refreshed annually; figures cited are from the latest editions as of mid-2026.

McK

McKinsey

QuantumBlack — McKinsey's AI arm.

Flagship free research

  • The State of AI — annual global survey
  • QuantumBlack Insights — articles & research
Takeaway: ~9 in 10 organisations use AI, but few have re-architected workflows enough to capture enterprise value — 2026 is the shift from pilots to operational responsibility.
BCG

BCG

Boston Consulting Group — AI strategy & BCG X.

Flagship free research

  • AI Insights hub — AI Radar & AI at Work surveys
  • The Leader's Guide to Transforming with AI
Takeaway: AI is now a CEO mandate, yet only ~1 in 4 firms report significant ROI — value comes from reshaping work, not just adding tools.
B&C

Bain

Bain & Company — the annual Technology Report.

Flagship free research

  • Technology Report — yearly AI & tech trends
  • Will AI disrupt tech's most valuable companies?
Takeaway: "AI leaders are extending their edge" — and meeting AI demand needs vast compute investment, with a projected ~$800bn data-centre funding gap.
Del

Deloitte

Deloitte AI Institute.

Flagship free research

  • State of AI in the Enterprise — multi-country survey
Takeaway: From 3,235 leaders across 24 countries: agentic AI is racing into the enterprise, and firms where leadership shapes AI governance capture significantly more value.
Acn

Accenture

Accenture — the annual Technology Vision.

Flagship free research

  • Technology Vision — yearly trend report
  • AI & Data — research & services
Takeaway: A "new age of autonomy" — 69% of executives say AI brings fresh urgency to reinventing how systems and processes are built.
PwC

PwC

PwC — AI strategy & the workforce.

Flagship free research

  • Global AI Jobs Barometer — a billion+ job ads
  • The Leadership Agenda: AI
Takeaway: AI is splitting the labour market into two tracks — and the most AI-exposed firms are raising wages and headcount faster, not slower.
